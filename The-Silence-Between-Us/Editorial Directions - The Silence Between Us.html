The ones it's for

The Silence Between Us explores the anxiety and depression that so many teenagers silently carry inside the school system — and the fear and uncertainty that prevent them from opening up to their own parents.

As emotional pressure builds and communication breaks down, one compassionate teacher becomes a guiding light, helping his students navigate some of the most difficult moments of their young lives. Through raw emotion and authentic storytelling, the film shines a light on trauma, isolation, healing, and the importance of being seen, heard, and understood.

“No story has mattered to me more than this one. These teenagers deserve to be seen, heard, and understood.”
