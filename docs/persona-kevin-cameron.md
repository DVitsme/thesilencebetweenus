## 1. Snapshot

Kevin Cameron is a Black, Canadian-born, Seventh-day Adventist **independent filmmaker** and
**storyteller** who left a career in education to make films full-time. He is confident, fast-moving,
faith-driven, and unapologetically entrepreneurial. He treats filmmaking as both ministry and
business — "this is what I do full time… the longer I wait, the less money I make" — and he funds
his own work rather than wait on gatekeepers. His brand voice is warm, exhortational, story-first,
and quietly proud. He sells *experiences and belonging*, not just tickets.

## 2. Origin story (the arc he tells about himself)

Kevin frames his life as a long, faithful climb from *good to great*:

- **Storyteller since boyhood.** "I have been a storyteller for a very long time… in high school I
  would always be performing skits for assembly at academy… even as an educator I was always making
  videos." He casts storytelling as a God-given calling: *"God put the burden on me to tell stories…
  Jesus was a storyteller; he communicated his message in parables."*
- **Came up through fashion, then education.** Known as "a fashion guy when he was younger," he
  became a school principal/teacher before answering "the call of God to get involved in media."
- **First film was humbling.** *Hush* (2009, 65 min): *"when I look back it was trash"* — said with a
  grin. The growth mindset is the point.
- **Paid his dues on other people's work.** Documentaries, commercials, promos for businesses and
  institutions — "my storytelling skills even with a small commercial was getting really good… and
  then I realized, you know what, I gotta stop doing other people's stuff and do my [own]."
- **Got burned by funding, then stopped waiting.** *For His Name's Sake* was conceived in Cleveland
  in 2013 — table read, fully cast — then "the funding fell through" and it sat on the shelf ~10 years.
  In Dec 2023 he and Roma revived it: *"what are we waiting for? Let's do this."* They **self-funded**:
  "I didn't wait [for] anybody to fund it… we funded it ourselves. We just did it."
- **Executes fast.** Filmed ~2–3 weeks (May 2024), premiered October. "Once I put my mind to
  something, I'm going to finish it. There was no pausing." "As an independent filmmaker, you got to
  get it done."

This arc — *talent → dues → setback → grit → ownership → execution → blessing* — is the emotional
spine of everything he says. Copy that echoes it will resonate.

## 3. Core values & worldview

1. **Faith first, integrated naturally.** God, calling, blessing, and ministry are woven through
   ordinary talk — never bolted on. Adventist markers: Sabbath/"after sunset," Oakwood, "Gift of
   Prophecy" class, church-hosted premieres. Films are ministry: "for his glory, for our good, and
   for others."
2. **Ownership & independence.** "I'm in charge. I can answer all the questions. It's my money."
   Being independent is a *point of pride*, not a limitation.
3. **Excellence over "Christian-cheap."** He resents the stereotype of low-quality faith films:
   his is "a real movie in every way," it "hits all the marks." He wants "the quality that I want."
4. **Grit / no as redirection.** "Life is filled with nos, but you have to keep going… you can't
   stop at a no." "Go from good to great."
5. **Time is money; momentum is sacred.** "The longer I wait, the less money I make." Speed and
   finishing are moral virtues to him.
6. **Family & community.** Family is central and visible; he leans on and credits his church
   community; success is shared.
7. **Black excellence & representation.** Proud of "a Black man doing this by himself," modeling for
   young, especially Christian, creators.
8. **Give back / co-labor.** Premieres double as fundraisers (Oakwood Academy 8th & 12th graders;
   NAACP). Support = "your time, your talents, or your treasures."

## 4. Personality & temperament

- **Confident, self-assured, a little swagger.** *"I am not new to this. I am true to this."*
  *"I've been doing this for a very long time — long before it became popular."*
- **Warm, funny, self-deprecating.** Family vlogs are gentle and observational (the running joke
  about his wife losing her phone — "I don't slow down… I know she's gonna find it"). The
  *"I'm a VIDEOGRAPHER!"* skit pokes fun at his own career change and his parents not getting it.
- **Mentor / exhorter.** He instinctively coaches: *"I admonish you to keep trying, keep putting out
  content. You have to put stuff out there."*
- **Decisive finisher.** Allergic to dithering; bias to action.
- **Generous host.** Frames everything as an *event* — red carpet, VIP, Q&A, food, "come looking
  good."
- **Grateful & humble-proud.** "I'm so blessed… so honored." Pride and humility coexist comfortably.

## 5. Motivations, goals & definition of success

- Build a **sustainable, full-time independent film business** (not a hobby, not waiting for Hollywood).
- Tell **meaningful, high-quality stories** — faith-rooted and socially aware (mental health, youth,
  family, perseverance through tragedy).
- **Fund and ship the next film** — currently *The Silence Between Us*.
- **Grow an audience and a supporter base** who feel like part of the journey.
- **Distribute via the church + theater circuit**, city by city ("the first of many in the US").
- Leave a **legacy** — "the first of many movies" — and **inspire the next generation** of Black
  Christian creators.
- Success looks like: a sold-out, dressed-up room that *loves the story*. "The most important thing
  to me about the premiere was that people love the **story**."

## 6. Pains, frustrations & dislikes (what to avoid in copy)

- **Waiting / delay / stalling.** "What are we waiting for?"
- **Dependence on gatekeepers' money** — the 2013 funding collapse still informs his self-funding ethos.
- **Mediocrity** — "good" that won't push to "great."
- **Cheap, inauthentic, "made-at-home" Christian films.** Never imply low production value.
- **Taking "no" as final.**
- **Doing "other people's stuff"** at the expense of his own art.
- **Freeloading / expecting art for free.** He (and his hosts) push back on the "I'm a member, I pay
  my tithe" mindset — *"you're going to do more than pay your tithe… you're going to buy a ticket…
  when good work like this happens we need to participate with more than just words of encouragement."*
  → Crucial for a payment site: **supporting a creator is participation, not charity, and not free.**
- **Being misunderstood about the creative path** (the parents skit) — a tender, relatable note.

## 8. Voice & tone profile — the heart of this guide

If you remember one thing: **Kevin talks like a confident storyteller-preacher-entrepreneur who is
also your warm, funny older cousin.** He builds momentum, lists reasons, repeats for emphasis,
addresses you directly, and lands on faith and invitation.

### How he actually sounds — patterns + evidence

- **Direct address + exhortation.** Speaks *to* you and tells you what to do.
  → "I admonish you to keep trying." · "You got to get it done." · "Please, I look forward to seeing you."
- **Enumerates benefits, fast.** "There's several reasons and I'll do it really quickly: one is…
  another thing is…" He *stacks* reasons to come/give.
- **Repetition for rhythm and conviction.** "We just did it, we just did it." · "It's fire… it's
  fire." · "Come on, come on." · "Yes sir, yes sir."
- **Aspirational contrasts.** "Good to great." · "Start somewhere to get where you're going." ·
  "More than just words of encouragement."
- **Confident self-positioning (earned, not boastful).** "I am not new to this, I am true to this." ·
  "Long before it became popular." · "I'm in charge… it's my money."
- **Faith woven in, conversational not preachy.** "God put the burden on me." · "God has so many
  ways… almost unexpected to us." · "For his glory, for our good, and for others." · "Blessed,"
  "honored," "anointed."
- **Story-first selling.** He pitches a film by *telling the story* and building suspense — "just
  imagine all the things going through his mind as he's on this journey" — then names the payoff
  ("you'll laugh, you'll cry, you'll be built up").
- **Sells the experience & belonging.** "It's a whole event." · "Date night… or come solo." ·
  "Come looking good." · "You're going to be the first… to premiere."
- **Inclusive, family-framed.** "A night out for *us*." · "Great for the family, great for the
  single, great for everybody."
- **Urgency + value of the work.** "The longer I wait, the less money I make." · Support is
  participation you *pay into*, gladly.
- **Gratitude as a close.** Thanks people by name, honors hosts and community, ends warm.

### Lexicon — words & phrases that are *his*

`fire` · `it's a whole event` · `family-friendly` · `date night` · `come looking good` ·
`good to great` · `put stuff out there` · `you got to get it done` · `we just did it` ·
`I'm in charge` · `the longer I wait, the less money I make` · `God put the burden on me` ·
`storyteller` · `for his glory, for our good, and for others` · `blessed` · `honored` ·
`riveting` · `inspired` · `I admonish you` · `not new to this, true to this` ·
`come on` · `yes sir` · `let's go` · `let's do this — what are we waiting for?`

### Do / Don't

| Do | Don't |
|---|---|
| Lead with the **story** and a human stakes question | Open with dry logistics or specs |
| Sound **confident and warm**, like a friend who's done this | Sound corporate, hedgy, or apologetic |
| Weave faith **naturally** | Preach, or strip faith out entirely |
| Frame support as **participation & belonging** | Frame it as charity, a "donation ask," or begging |
| Use **momentum**: short lines, lists, repetition, a clear CTA | Long, flat, passive paragraphs |
| Promise an **experience** (access, behind-the-scenes, credit) | Promise only "a movie" |
| Signal **quality/excellence** | Anything that hints "low-budget" |
| **Honor** the supporter/community by name | Generic, impersonal thank-yous |

## 9. How he markets (relevant to the payment-capture goal)

Kevin already runs a repeatable, effective playbook — the site should systematize it:

- **Tiered access & VIP.** He naturally offers VIP perks, swag, Q&A, red carpet → maps directly to
  **supporter tiers** (the JotForm's Tier 1 = $175 is the seed of this).
- **Experience > transaction.** People pay for the *night*, the *belonging*, the *story* — and to
  *back a creator they believe in*.
- **Cause partnerships.** Premieres fund schools/nonprofits; supporting the film also supports
  something bigger. Bake a "your support does more" thread into copy.
- **Scarcity & sequence.** "First US city," "first of many," limited premieres → urgency.
- **Social proof & numbers.** "400 people at the premiere," sold-out, multi-city tour, audience
  reactions → use real numbers and testimonials.
- **Frictionless, multi-rail payment.** He already uses Cash App / Zelle / Eventbrite + QR codes.
  The redesign's job: **capture payment smoothly on-site** while keeping his trusted rails available.
- **He fronts the camera.** Expect (and design for) Kevin's own video pitch as a hero element.

**Audience he's speaking to (so copy speaks *from him, to them*):** Black Christian families and
church communities (esp. SDA), date-night & family-night seekers, mission-minded givers, and
aspiring filmmakers who admire the independent path. They move for: inspiration, authenticity &
quality, family-friendliness, *being part of something*, and giving back.

## 10. Applying the voice to "The Silence Between Us"

Confirmed synopsis (verbatim from the funding form):

> *"The Silence Between Us is a powerful and necessary film that explores anxiety and depression
> many teenagers silently face within the school system, and the fear and uncertainty that often
> prevent them from opening up to their parents."*

A **compassionate teacher becomes a guiding light**, helping students through the hardest moments of
their young lives. Themes: trauma, isolation, healing, parent–child communication breakdown,
mental-health awareness in adolescents.

This is **deeply personal** for Kevin: he was a school **principal/teacher** before film, and his
documentary work already centers at-risk youth (the Cleveland piece). It sits right at the
intersection of his faith-film heart and his social-issue lens. The throughline he'll want:
**hard truth + hope + "you're not alone."** Tonally: honest about the struggle, never hopeless;
redemptive without being saccharine.

### Sample on-voice copy (drafts — to make the persona "click")

**Hero**
> **There's a silence between us. This film breaks it.**
> *The Silence Between Us* is an honest, hope-filled story about what our young people carry — and
> what happens when somebody finally listens. I've been telling stories my whole life, and this is
> the one I can't stay quiet about. Let's make it together.

**Why this film (his voice)**
> I'm not new to this — I'm true to this. I make real films, family-friendly films, films that leave
> you inspired. But more than that: too many kids are fighting battles in silence, and God put this
> story on my heart. I'm not waiting for permission to tell it. The question is simple — *what are we
> waiting for?*

**Become a Supporter (reframing the ask as participation)**
> Good work like this only happens when we participate — with more than words. When you back *The
> Silence Between Us*, you're not donating; you're **producing**. You get a front-row seat to the
> journey: behind-the-scenes access, your name in the credits, the premiere experience. Let's go.

**Supporter tier CTA (e.g., Tier 1 — $175)**
> **Founding Supporter — $175.** On-screen "Supporter" credit · virtual meet & greet with the
> director and cast · a permanent spot on the Founding Supporters page · quarterly updates from set ·
> early trailer access. Come be part of it. *Yes sir — let's build.*

**Thank-you (post-payment)**
> I see you, and I'm grateful. You didn't just give — you joined the family behind this film. We're
> going to make something that matters, and you'll have helped birth it. Blessed to have you.
> stay tuned — the journey's just getting started.

> These are starting points, not final copy. The test for every line: *would Kevin say it out loud,
> just like that?* If it sounds corporate, cut it. If it sounds like a confident, faithful
> storyteller inviting you to build something with him — keep it.
